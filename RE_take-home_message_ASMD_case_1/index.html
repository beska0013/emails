Acid sphingomyelinase deficiency (ASMD) s an autosomal recessive disease that can occur in both adults and children, caused by pathogenic variants in SMPD1 .

There are three disease types: A (infantile neurovisceral), A/B (chronic neurovisceral), and B (chronic visceral).

Common signs of ASMD include hepatosplenomegaly, abnormal liver function tests, atherogenic lipid profile, lung disease, cytopenia, skeletal abnormalities, and growth/puberty delay.

  • Manifestations overlap with other lysosomal storage disorders, particularly Gaucher disease, contributing to underdiagnosis and diagnostic delay.

ASMD type A/B

  • Progressive disease course, including neurological degeneration which is milder than in ASMD type A.
  • Patients experience substantial disease burden, and may die prematurely from ASMD complications in childhood/adulthood.
  • Associated morbidities can significantly impact QoL and psychosocial aspects of affected children, including limited physical activity, social isolation, and peer rejection.

Diagnosis confirmed by the detection of deficient ASM enzyme activity and presence of biallelic pathogenic variants in SMPD1 on molecular genetic testing.

  • Parallel testing of glucocerebrosidase enzyme activity (Gaucher disease) is recommended.

Therapeutic interventions are aimed at symptom management.

Patients and relatives may be informed of the options available for addressing ASMD manifestations, and receive genetic testing and counseling.

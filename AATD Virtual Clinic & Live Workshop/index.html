Overview :

Alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency (AATD) is a rare genetic condition characterized by low circulating levels of AAT.1 Patients with AATD are at an increased risk of liver disease, presenting in infancy/adulthood, and/or lung disease, typically in individuals over the age of 30, with COPD being the most common clinical manifestation of AATD in adults.1,2

Under-recognition of AATD often causes diagnostic delay, with many individuals being symptomatic for several years and visiting many physicians before an initial diagnosis of AATD.3 Delay in diagnosis has been shown to be associated with worsened clinical status at the time of diagnosis.3

This webinar focuses on the diagnosis and management of AATD, and how best practice and current guidelines may be used to improve patient outcomes.

Acid sphingomyelinase deficiency (ASMD) is an autosomal recessive disease that can occur in both adults and children, caused by pathogenic variants in SMPD1 .

It has three disease types: A (infantile neurovisceral), A/B (chronic neurovisceral), and B (chronic visceral).

Common signs of ASMD include hepatosplenomegaly, liver dysfunction, atherogenic lipid profile, lung disease, cytopenias, and skeletal abnormalities.

Symptoms overlap with those of other lysosomal storage diseases, particularly Gaucher disease, contributing to underdiagnosis and diagnostic delay.

  • Mixed dyslipidemia and the presence of interstitial lung disease (ILD) are suggestive findings more clearly associated with ASMD.

ASMD type B

  • Progressive disease course without neurological involvement, and more frequent pulmonary involvement (mainly ILD) than other ASMD types.
  • Patients experience substantial disease burden, and may die prematurely from ASMD complications, mainly respiratory and liver disease.
  • Adults may experience compromised work ability, decreased QoL, limited physical activity, chronic fatigue, and social isolation.

Diagnosis confirmed by the detection of deficient ASM enzyme activity and presence of biallelic pathogenic variants in SMPD1 on molecular genetic testing.

  • Parallel testing of glucocerebrosidase enzyme activity (Gaucher disease) is recommended.

Therapeutic interventions are aimed at symptom management.

Patients and relatives may be informed of the options available for addressing ASMD manifestations, and receive genetic testing and counseling.
